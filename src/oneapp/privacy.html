OneStory • Privacy

Privacy Policy

This page explains what information OneStory collects, how it’s used, and the choices you have.

App: OneStory Effective date: 2025-12-21 Page: /onestory/privacy

What we collect

OneStory does not ask for your name, email, or account. It fetches story content from Firebase Firestore and stores a small “last read” record on your device.

What we don’t do

No ads SDK, no third‑party analytics SDK, and no selling of personal data. If this changes, we’ll update this policy.

1) Summary

  • Stories: delivered from our backend using Google Firebase Firestore.
  • Local progress: we store the last story you read (story ID, date, timestamp) locally on your device to improve your experience.
  • App security: Firebase App Check may be used to help protect the backend from abuse.

2) Information we collect

OneStory is designed to work without creating an account.

  • Story content requests: when the app loads stories, your device connects to Firebase Firestore. Like most internet services, this may include standard server logs and network metadata (for example, IP address and request timing) handled by our service providers.
  • Local “last read” record (on-device): OneStory stores:
    • lastStoryId (the last story you opened)
    • storyDate (YYYY‑MM‑DD)
    • lastStoryReadAt (timestamp)

3) How we use information

  • Provide the app: load and display the daily stories.
  • Improve UX: remember the last story you read and show “read” state.
  • Protect services: reduce abusive traffic (e.g., via Firebase App Check).

4) Sharing

We do not sell your personal information.

  • Service providers: OneStory uses Google Firebase (Firestore and related infrastructure) to deliver story content. Data processed by Firebase is subject to Google’s terms and privacy practices.
  • Legal: we may disclose information if required by law or to protect users and the service.

5) Data retention

  • On-device: the local “last read” record remains on your device until you uninstall the app or clear its data.
  • Backend: story content is hosted in Firestore; retention follows operational needs and provider policies.

6) Security

We use reasonable safeguards to protect the service. No method of transmission or storage is 100% secure, but we work to minimize risk.

7) Children’s privacy

OneStory is not intended for children under 13. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13.

8) Your choices

  • Delete local data: uninstall the app or clear app storage to remove the on-device “last read” record.
  • Contact us: you can request help or ask privacy questions using the email below.

9) Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. We will post the latest version on this page and update the “Effective date” above.

10) Contact
